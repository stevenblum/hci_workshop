Human-Computer Interaction Workshop

Lesson materials for studying human-centered design, task analysis, human performance, visual attention, cognitive load, and applied evaluation of complex systems.

Lessons

3 available, 5 planned

  1. Lesson 0

    Introduction and Case Studies

    Establish the workshop frame for human-centered evaluation, then connect HCI concepts to applied examples in control rooms, driving, manufacturing, and healthcare.

    Open presentation

  2. Lesson 1

    Cognitive Models

    Model user tasks with hierarchical task analysis and GOMS/KLM methods, then use those models to predict performance and identify interface improvements.

    Open presentation

  3. Lesson 2

    Visual Attention and Eye Tracking

    Use visual attention theory, eye-movement measures, and AOI-based analysis to evaluate interfaces and human performance in complex operational settings.

    Open presentation

  4. Lesson 3

    Mental Models and Decision Making

    Analyze how users form expectations about systems, interpret feedback, and make decisions under uncertainty in operational interfaces.

    • Mental models and interface expectations
    • Situation awareness and feedback loops
    • Decision support and automation bias
    • Errors, slips, mistakes, and recovery
    Planned

  5. Lesson 4

    Cognitive Load

    Evaluate how interface complexity, task switching, information density, and time pressure shape workload, attention, and user performance.

    • Intrinsic, extraneous, and germane load
    • Secondary-task and dual-task measures
    • NASA-TLX and subjective workload ratings
    • Design strategies for reducing load
    Planned

  6. Lesson 5

    EEG and Physiological Measures

    Use physiological signals to study cognitive state, workload, stress, and engagement during interaction with complex human-machine systems.

    • EEG indicators of attention and workload
    • Heart rate variability and electrodermal activity
    • Signal quality, artifacts, and synchronization
    • Multimodal measures for interface evaluation
    Planned

  7. Lesson 6

    Neural Networks for Predicting HCI

    Build predictive models from interaction, behavioral, and physiological data to estimate user state, performance, and interface risk.

    • Feature engineering for HCI datasets
    • Sequence models for gaze and interaction logs
    • Performance, workload, and error prediction
    • Model validation, interpretability, and limits
    Planned

  8. Lesson 7

    Human Interaction with AI

    Evaluate AI-enabled interfaces by studying trust, transparency, control, collaboration, and human oversight in decision-making workflows.

    • Trust calibration and reliance
    • Explainability and uncertainty communication
    • Human-AI teaming and shared control
    • Evaluation methods for AI-assisted work
    Planned
